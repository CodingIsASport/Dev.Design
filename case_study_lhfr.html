Liberty Heights Fire & Rescue

A comprehensive, professional branding and asset kit designed for a realism-based ERLC server. Focused on authority, trust, and a cohesive visual identity.

Role: Lead Designer
Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, Roblox Studio
Timeline: 2 Weeks

Project Overview

Liberty Heights Fire & Rescue needed an identity that felt rooted in reality. The goal was to step away from generic server assets and build a brand that commanded respect, looking as accurate and polished as a real-world municipal fire department. The project spanned across vehicle liveries, custom patches, uniform texturing, and digital community assets (Discord).

Mission Statement

"Serving Liberty Heights with professionalism, integrity, and unwavering dedication to life safety."

Color Palette

A commanding palette relying on deep reds and high-contrast whites & yellows to emulate real-world high-visibility fire apparatus.

Vehicle Livery Design

The fleet design was crucial. Engine and Ladder trucks feature a deep red base with a bold white horizontal stripe and yellow reflective striping underneath. The styling emphasizes high visibility while maintaining a clean, modern aesthetic. Support vehicles, like the Battalion Chief SUV, utilize a sleek black base with a subtle red side fade to differentiate command staff from line apparatus.

Uniform & Rank Structure

The uniform package was designed to visually separate the chain of command effortlessly. Station wear consists of breathable black polos with red accent lines and embroidered logos. Command staff are distinguished by formal white button-downs and gold insignia.

The Rank Structure included specific badges and striping for: Probationary, Firefighter, Senior Firefighter, Lieutenant, Captain, Battalion Chief, Assistant Chief, and Fire Chief.

Digital Integration

To bring the community together, the branding extended to the department's Discord and documentation. This included a unified server icon, matching role colors syncing directly with the in-game rank structure (e.g., Red for firefighters, Gold for chiefs), and standardizing social media templates for announcements, promos, and recruitment drives.
